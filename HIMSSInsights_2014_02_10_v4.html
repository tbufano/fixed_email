STOP THE PATIENT!

ARE WE OVERDOING PATIENT ENGAGEMENT?

Have we fallen into a trap where we assume that all patient engagement in eHealth is always positive? Is there a danger, meanwhile, that citizens armed with the latest health apps can end up with too much information for their own good?

These are some fascinating and provocative questions, sparked by the news that some policy makers want to pull back from the much-vaunted ‘patient 2.0’ model we’ve all been hearing about.

If, like us, you think these are serious issues that need intelligent comment and analysis, then please go to our cover story in the latest issue of HIMSS’ independent health IT journal, HIMSS Insights, where it is the lead feature. You can also explore the following topical articles:

  • To coincide with the fact HIMSS’ first major event in France, World of Health IT (WoHIT14), is happening this Spring, we offer a bird’s eye view of the state of healthcare in this major global economy.
  • Is it time to think again about the endless growth of medical ‘apps’ – that may, in fact, do more harm than good?
  • Explore the impact of new ways of sharing biomedical research: ‘PubMed.’ Will it change the way scientists work?
  • Could recent advances in genomic science bring the idea of truly predictive medicine into the realm of practical healthcare?
Get in touch with your views!
